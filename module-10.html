Module 10: Graphic Design in Everyday Life

Design chosen: Trivago ad (“OH YEAH? PROVE IT!”)

Image

Design elements, relationships, and gestalt principles

  • Contrast: White letters on a darker background makes the message easy to read fast.
  • Hierarchy: The slogan is the main focus; the brand name is smaller, so you read the message first.
  • Layout: The text sits across the bottom and anchors the image, almost like a headline.
  • Figure-ground (Gestalt): The man and the large text stand out from the background, so your eyes lock onto them.
  • Proximity (Gestalt): The words are grouped tightly together, so they read as one strong challenge.
  • Attention/focal point: The man’s face + the bold slogan create a clear focal point.

What it’s appealing to / what action it wants

This ad appeals to a feeling of skepticism and confidence—like someone is challenging a claim and asking for proof. The message “OH YEAH? PROVE IT!” makes you curious and pushes you to verify prices instead of trusting one option. The action it encourages is to use Trivago to compare hotel prices and feel like you’re making a smarter choice.
